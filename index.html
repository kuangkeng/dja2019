This year, the Data Journalism Awards received a total of 607 submissions from all over the world.
(The initial number is 608 but one submission was later withdrawn by the author)
A large number of submissions (40%) flocked in during the last 3 days before deadline. Another evidence that journalists like to do things at the eleventh hour.
They come from 62 countries/areas, the largest number of countries/area ever represented since the competition started in 2012.
A big surprise this year is that for the first time Asia has overtaken Europe as the region that submitted the highest number of entries, contributing almost one third or 181 of the 607 entries.
Two-third of the submissions (395 out of 607) come from large organisations with more than 30 editorial members. The rest are from small organisations.
Among the 12 categories, "Data visualisation of the Year" and "Award for innovation in data journalism" attracted the highest number of submissions.
Out of all the 607 submissions, the pre-jury has picked 103 submissions for the shortlist.
While Asian organisations contributes the most submissions, those from the North America and Europe made up most of the shortlist.
From the shortlist, the jury members will select the final winners. They will be announced at the ceremony and gala dinner held during the GEN Summit in Athens, Greece, on 15 June 2019.
